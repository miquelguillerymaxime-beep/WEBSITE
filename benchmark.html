Retrieval · 4,475 real decisions, 400 paraphrased queries

Training-free fusion beats either half.

Queries are paraphrased into synonyms, so neither keyword nor embedding gets a free lexical hit. Vector-only underperforms on identifier-heavy code; fusing keyword and vector wins without any training.

Vector alone underperforms keyword on code. Fusion beats both. A cross-encoder lifts list recall but leaves R@1 near 0.70.

Honestly stated On plain retrieval, just find the topic, we are not a clear winner: a tuned BM25 ties us on recall. Our edge is picking the right node with its history and dependency path, not raw neighbor finding. We make no claim to beat conversational-memory systems, they answer a different question. Where we separate is the structural answer: what calls what, what breaks, and what was true when.
