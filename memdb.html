Why · time is the point

A record that remembers when it was true.

Two clocks, not one. Every fact carries a decision time, when it was recorded, and a valid time, the interval over which it actually held. Most databases keep only the latest value. MemDB keeps the whole timeline.

Ask as-of any moment. "What did rotateToken call in March?" returns the value that was valid in March, even though it was superseded in May. The current head does not overwrite the past, it supersedes it.

Nothing is ever deleted. Superseded facts are demoted, not destroyed. Perfect recall for audit and provenance stays available while the active set stays small and fast.
